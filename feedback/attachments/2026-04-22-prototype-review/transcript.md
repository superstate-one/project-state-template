**Kaloian:** Let's start with the portfolio dashboard — what's your first
impression?

**Client:** I like the layout, it's clear. But this field here — apartment

**Kaloian:** Good catch. That needs to be a free text field. We'll fix that.

**Client:** Yeah, because nothing is ever round. 28 apartments, 47, 133 on

**Kaloian:** Noted. What about the income column?

**Client:** That's exactly what I wanted to see. Monthly income per building.
Can you make it add up at the top? Like a total?

**Kaloian:** Yes, that's easy. What about the property manager I mentioned
at the end of our first call — do you want them to have access?

**Client:** Actually, yes, I forgot about that. He handles the day-to-day
for three buildings — talking to tenants, collecting rent, that kind of thing.
He doesn't need to see my whole portfolio, just his buildings. Can you make
a separate view for him?

**Kaloian:** Absolutely. So they handle the operations, you handle the
investment decisions.

**Client:** Exactly. He shouldn't see my financial overview or what the other
buildings earn. Just his three.

**Kaloian:** Makes sense. Could you collect rent through the app? Would that
be useful?

**Client:** That would be great. Through Stripe? I don't know how these
things work. Whatever is simplest and cheapest. Just — can the tenant get
a reminder and pay somehow?

**Kaloian:** We'll figure out the payment side. One more thing — what happens
if an apartment doesn't have a rent amount set yet? Like when you first add it?

**Client:** It should show as empty, not as zero. Zero would mean the
apartment is free, which is wrong.
